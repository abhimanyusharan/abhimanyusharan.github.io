Analysis and Optimization of Heat Exchangers
Oct 2017-May 2019
2 Publications
Final Year B.Tech Project
  • The aim of the project was to analyse a three-fluid heat exchanger using Galerkin's finite element method in terms of effectiveness-number of transfer units plots.
  • It was initially done for 4 different arrangements of 3-fluid cross-flow heat exchangers and the one showing the best results was considered for further investigation.
  • The considered exchanger was then optimized for a given set of fluid parameters to determine the optimal dimensions for high effectiveness and low pressure drop. This was done using Genetic Algorithhm and using Particle Swarm Optimization. This has been published in a journal as follows- Jyothiprakash, K. H., Harshith, J., Sharan, A., Seetharamu, K. N., & Krishnegowda, Y. T. (2019). Thermodynamic Optimization of Three-Fluid Cross-Flow Heat Exchanger Using GA and PSO Heuristics. Thermal Science and Engineering Progress, 11, 289–301. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tsep.2019.04.009
  • Further analysis was carried out for the heat exchanger by including other effects such as heat interaction with the surroundings and heat loss due to conduction in the seperating walls of the heat exchanger. I have presented this at a conference called THERMACOMP-2018, which was held at Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore. The proceedings can be seen here (Refer to page 55 for my paper). Jyothiprakash, K. H., Sharan, A., Harshith, J., Krishna, V., Seetharamu, K. N., & Krishnegowda, Y. T. (2018). Combined effect of ambient heat-in-leak and longitudinal wall conduction on the performance of three-fluid cross-flow heat exchanger. In 5th International Conference on Computational Methods for Thermal Problems (pp. 55–58).
  • The analysis was also carried out for two-phase flow.
  • Skill and Experience Acquired: MATLAB, Finite Element Analysis
    The entire project was hard coded on MATLAB without the help of any commercially available analysis tool. It included converting the governing equations to stiffness matrices, simulating it to observe element wise temperature distribution, and generating the results for various conditions.
